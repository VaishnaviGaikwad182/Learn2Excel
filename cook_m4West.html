Basundi

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter full cream milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 8-10 almonds (sliced)
  • 8-10 pistachios (sliced)
  • 1 tbsp chopped cashews
  • 8-10 saffron strands (soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk)
  • 1 tbsp chironji (optional)

Recipe:

  1. In a heavy-bottomed pan, bring the milk to a boil, then reduce the flame to low.
  2. Keep stirring frequently to prevent it from sticking to the bottom.
  3. Let the milk reduce to about half its original quantity.
  4. Add sugar, saffron milk, and cardamom powder. Stir well.
  5. Add almonds, pistachios, cashews, and chironji. Cook for another 5-7 minutes.
  6. Cool the basundi and serve chilled or warm, garnished with more nuts.

Puri Bhaji

Ingredients:

For Puri:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 tbsp semolina (optional, for crispiness)
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Water (for kneading)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

For Bhaji (Potato Sabzi):

  • 4 medium potatoes (boiled and cubed)
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 1-2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/4 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander (for garnish)
  • 1/2 lemon (for a tangy finish)

Recipe:

  • For Puri:
    1. In a bowl, mix wheat flour, semolina, and salt. Add water gradually to knead a firm dough.
    2. Divide into small balls, roll each into a small circle (not too thin).
    3. Heat oil in a kadhai. Deep fry each puri on medium-high flame until puffed and golden.
    4. Drain excess oil on paper towels.
  • For Bhaji:
    1. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and let them crackle.
    2. Add curry leaves, green chilies, and hing. Sauté sliced onions till translucent.
    3. Add turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
    4. Add boiled potatoes, mash lightly, and mix everything evenly. Simmer for 5 minutes.
    5. Finish with fresh coriander and a squeeze of lemon juice.

Dal - Bati

Ingredients:

For Bati:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup semolina (rava)
  • 4 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/2 tsp carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • Warm water (for kneading)

For Dal:

  • 1/2 cup toor dal (pigeon peas)
  • 1/4 cup moong dal (yellow lentils)
  • 1/4 cup chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/4 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander (for garnish)

Recipe:

For Bati:

  1. Mix wheat flour, semolina, ghee, ajwain, baking soda, and salt.
  2. Knead into a stiff dough using warm water.
  3. Divide into medium balls, flatten slightly.
  4. Bake at 180°C for 30 minutes or cook in a bati oven until golden brown.
  5. Dip each bati in melted ghee before serving.

For Dal:

  1. Pressure cook toor dal, moong dal, and chana dal with turmeric and salt for 3-4 whistles.
  2. Heat ghee in a pan, add cumin seeds, asafoetida, ginger-garlic paste, and green chilies. Sauté.
  3. Add tomatoes, chili powder, coriander powder, and cook till tomatoes soften.
  4. Add the boiled dal, mix well, and simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Garnish with fresh coriander.
