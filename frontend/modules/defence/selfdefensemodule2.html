Master Basic Strikes & Blocks in Self-Defense

Objectives

  • Understand the importance of self-defense and personal safety.
  • Learn basic striking techniques like punches, kicks, and elbow strikes.
  • Master defensive blocking techniques to protect against attacks.
  • Develop situational awareness and quick reflexes.
  • Practice real-life self-defense scenarios to enhance decision-making.

Introduction to Self-Defense

Self-defense is about awareness, prevention, and using effective techniques to protect yourself. Learning basic strikes and blocks will help you handle threats safely.

Basic Blocks (Defensive Techniques)

  • High Block: Protects your head from overhead strikes by raising your arm above your head.
  • Low Block: Shields your legs and lower body from incoming kicks by moving your arm downward.
  • Inside Block: Redirects attacks aimed at your torso by sweeping your arm inward.
  • Outside Block: Deflects attacks moving toward your sides by moving your arm outward.
  • Parry: A quick deflective motion to redirect an attacker's strike.

Basic Strikes (Offensive Techniques)

  • Elbow Strike: A powerful close-range attack using your elbow.
  • Palm Strike: A safe and effective strike using the base of your palm.
  • Front Kick: A direct kick aimed at the attacker's midsection to push them away.
  • Knee Strike: A strong knee movement targeting the attacker's groin or abdomen.

Escapes, Defense Against Grabs & Weapon Defense

Escapes from Holds:

    Wrist Grab Escape:
    • If someone grabs your wrist, rotate your hand towards the weakest point of their grip (usually between the thumb and fingers) and pull away quickly.
    • Use your free hand or a kick to create space for an escape.
    Bear Hug Escape:
    • If grabbed from behind, drop your weight low and use your elbows to strike the attacker's ribs or groin.
    • Step to the side while twisting your body to break free.
    Headlock Escape:
    • Tuck your chin to protect your neck.
    • Strike the attacker's groin or ribs while using your hands to push against their grip.
    • Move sideways and slip out of the hold.

Defense Against Grabs:

    Clothing Grab Defense:
    • If an attacker grabs your shirt, use both hands to push their hand down while stepping back to create distance.
    • Follow up with a counterattack (e.g., palm strike or knee strike).
    Chokehold Defense:
    • If choked from the front, raise your arms and use both hands to break the grip while stepping back.

Weapon Defense:

    Knife Defense:
    • If an attacker has a knife, try to create distance rather than engaging directly.
    • If close, control the attacker's wrist and redirect the weapon while using strikes to disable them.
    Gun Threat Defense:
    • If an attacker points a gun at close range, avoid sudden movements.
    • Look for an opportunity to redirect the weapon, control their wrist, and disarm them using leverage.

Key Defensive Moves:

  • Elbow Strike: A powerful close-range attack using your elbow to disable the attacker.
  • Palm Strike: A safe and effective strike using the base of your palm to target the attacker's chin or nose.
  • Front Kick: A direct kick aimed at the attacker's midsection to push them away.
  • Knee Strike: A strong knee movement targeting the attacker's groin or abdomen.
