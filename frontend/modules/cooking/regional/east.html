Mishti Doi

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter full cream milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (for caramel)
  • 1/4 cup sugar (for sweetness)
  • 2 tbsp curd (for fermentation)

Recipe:

  1. Heat milk and reduce it to 3/4th of its original quantity.
  2. In a separate pan, caramelize 1/2 cup sugar until golden. Add this to the milk and stir until fully dissolved.
  3. Add the remaining sugar and let the milk cool to lukewarm temperature.
  4. Whisk in the curd and mix well.
  5. Pour into clay pots for an authentic touch and cover.
  6. Let it ferment for 6-8 hours or overnight in a warm place.
  7. Chill before serving for a rich, creamy delight!

Dum Aloo

Ingredients:

  • 8-10 small potatoes (boiled and peeled)
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup yogurt (whisked)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1 tbsp cashew paste (optional for creaminess)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Recipe:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and lightly fry the boiled potatoes until golden brown. Set aside.
  2. In the same pan, add more oil if needed, sauté onions until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Add tomato puree, turmeric, chili powder, cumin, coriander powder, and salt. Cook until the masala thickens and oil separates.
  4. Lower the heat, add whisked yogurt gradually, stirring continuously to avoid curdling.
  5. Stir in cashew paste (optional) for richness. Add the fried potatoes and mix well.
  6. Cover and cook on low heat ("dum" style) for 10 minutes, letting the potatoes soak up the flavors.
  7. Finish with garam masala, crushed kasuri methi, and fresh coriander.

Fish Curry

Ingredients:

  • 500g fish (Rohu, Catla, or Pomfret work well)
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk (optional for a coastal touch)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds (methi)
  • 1 tbsp mustard oil (for an authentic flavor)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Recipe:

  1. Marinate the fish with turmeric, chili powder, and salt. Let it rest for 15 minutes.
  2. Heat mustard oil in a pan until smoky. Fry the fish lightly on both sides and set aside.
  3. In the same oil, add mustard seeds and fenugreek seeds. Let them splutter.
  4. Sauté onions until golden brown, then add ginger-garlic paste and cook until aromatic.
  5. Add tomato puree, turmeric, chili powder, cumin, coriander powder, and salt. Cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  6. Add a splash of water if needed, then pour in coconut milk (optional). Simmer the curry for 5 minutes.
  7. Gently place the fried fish into the curry. Let it cook on low flame for 7-8 minutes.
  8. Finish with garam masala, lemon juice, and fresh coriander.
