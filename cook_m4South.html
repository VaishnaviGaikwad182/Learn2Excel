Idli-Sambar-Coconut Chutney

Ingredients:

  • For Idli:
    • 2 cups idli rice
    • 1 cup urad dal (husked black gram)
    • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds
    • Salt to taste
    • Water as needed
  • For Sambar:
    • 1 cup toor dal (pigeon peas)
    • 1 onion (sliced)
    • 1 tomato (chopped)
    • 2-3 green chilies
    • 1 cup sliced Red pumpkin
    • 1 tbsp of ginger-garlic and coconut paste
    • 2 drum-sticks chopped into pieces
    • 2 tbsp tamarind pulp
    • 1 tsp grated jaggery
    • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
    • 1 tsp red chili powder
    • 1 tbsp sambar powder
    • Salt to taste
    • 1/2 tsp hing
    • 2 tbsp oil
  • For Coconut Chutney:
    • 1/2 cup grated coconut
    • 2 tbsp soaked chana dal
    • 2-3 green chilis
    • 1-2 cloves of ginger-garlic
    • 1 tsp mustard seeds
    • 1 tsp cumin seeds
    • 1/4 tsp hing
    • Salt to taste
    • Water as needed

Recipe:

  • For Idli:
    1. Soak rice and urad dal separately (fenugreek seeds with dal) for 6 hours.
    2. Grind dal to a smooth batter and rice to a coarse batter.
    3. Mix both, add salt, and ferment overnight.
    4. Grease idli molds, pour batter, and steam for 10-12 minutes.
  • For Sambar:
    1. In a pressure cooker, combine toor dal, chana dal, onion, tomato, green chilies, pumpkin, drumsticks, and turmeric powder.
    2. Add 3 cups water and salt. Pressure cook for 3-4 whistles until dal and veggies are soft.
    3. Once pressure releases, mash the dal lightly, leaving some texture.
    4. Heat oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, dried chilies, hing, and curry leaves. Let it crackle.
    5. Add sambar powder, red chili powder, jaggery, tamarind pulp, and ginger-garlic-coconut paste. Mix well and simmer for 5-7 minutes.
    6. Pour the sambar and mix well.
    7. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot with rice, idli, dosa, or appe.
  • For Coconut Chutney:
    1. Grind coconut, chana dal, green chilis, ginger-garlic, coriander, curd and salt to a smooth paste.
    2. Prepare tempering — heat oil, crackle mustard seeds, cumin seeds, curry leaves.
    3. Pour the tempering over chutney and mix well.

Dosa-Sambar-Chutney

Ingredients:

  • Same batter as idli (slightly thinner consistency)
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

Recipe:

  1. Heat a tawa, lightly grease.
  2. Pour a ladle of batter, spread in a circular motion.
  3. Drizzle oil/ghee around edges, cook till crispy.
  4. Serve hot with chutney and sambar.

Appe-Chutney

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 cups idli/dosa batter
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 green chili (finely chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • A few curry leaves (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp coriander leaves (chopped)
  • Oil for frying

Recipe:

  1. Heat oil, splutter mustard seeds, sauté onions, chili, curry leaves.
  2. Mix the tempering into the batter, add coriander leaves.
  3. Heat appe pan, grease, pour batter into molds.
  4. Cook till golden brown on both sides, flipping halfway.
